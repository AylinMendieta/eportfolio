Mystery Black Box Investigation

By: Aylin Mendieta Yahuitl

Background Information About The Mystery Black Box

In the New Media and Tech. for Social Change class, students are trying to experiment, develop, and create different methods to explain their thoughts about what is inside the mystery box. The mystery box is a box that was built in Mr. Sinusas’s class. The mystery box causes the amount of liquid to change. Our goal during this investigation was to know what was causing the amount of water to change. We also were trying to design a similar box trying to imitate the original. We collected data to analyze how it change.

Claim

Inside of the box there is a container connected with a tube that is positioned after 1000mL of water.

Evidence

The original mystery box causes the amount of water to change. For example we pour 1000mL and it change into 712mL. We notice that it took a lot for the water to come out. Also we notice that the water would take more or less time depending on the amount.

Our mystery box trials worked perfectly. Our box took two days to get final design, some of our first attempts failed because the materials were position wrong or we didn’t have enough glue. We had less water and more water in every trial. For example we pour 1000mL and it change into 870mL.

Reasoning, Part 1

Models are designs created by us or others to give an idea of how a prototype will work. Models are useful to represent an idea to then turn it into a physical or we may design it in computer to collect data or just test it. Our limits are that our models may not be accurate and may affect results. Making a physical project it’s difficult because we don't get the same result as we were expecting to get.

Reasoning, Part 2

Each time that we pour 1000mL exact nothing happens even if we wait for a hole hour. When we add more, the water comes out. Our evidence is correct because we pour an exact amount that was not exactly 1000mL but we try to get close so it wouldn’t come out as the real mystery box. It happened the same thing that happens when we pour 1000mL into the mystery box meaning that our thoughts were correct.

Reasoning, Part 3

Inside the mystery box there is a box that when it gets to a certain weight it makes water to come out and then it closes. Our time variable would have change into a weight variable. We would have gotten different numbers, more high numbers than less numbers. It would have change the hole procedure and affect the model because we would have need to use different materials and connect them in way that the prototype would work without no one touching it.

3D Modeling, Animation, and Augmented Reality

We use the Maya program to model, design, and animated our mystery black box. The project started with a snowman since we didn't know how to use the program. The next class we started the box modeling in Maya. We started with basic shapes to start the box, then we started to use different tools such as extrude to give shape to the objects. Then we design the tube and make the hole to were the tube would go through. We added color, and the final step was animation. The animation was easy but if you made a mistake you would have to do it again till it looks as you want it to look. It was harder when modeling the water, but I wasn't able to get to that part since I was behind.

Using augmented reality was also easy we just had to download the box that we made in maya in a program called Merge Edu. In Merge Edu we labeled our project and gather more information about it, to then create a QR code that would help us get a 3D model. When matching the same QR code to a cube that would help us see our box from every angle depending on the cube position. That was all the project we did, and finally here are some examples of practice models that we use to show our knowledge and also the mystery box.
